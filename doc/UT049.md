**Does existential negation have a special negator?**

The negative strategies used for standard negation (i.e., negation of declarative verbal main clauses) often differ from stategies used in existentials, imperatives, and nonverbal clauses (Miestamo 2007). This question investigates whether existentials require a special negative marker not found with standard negation. For instance, the Hungarian *nincs* occurs in existentials (1a), whereas *nem* is used for standard negation (1b).

(1) Hungarian<br/>
>a. ***Nincs**  igazság a  Földön*<br/>
Nincs  igazság a  Föld-ön*<br/>
>NEG justice ART world-ADE<br/>
>‘There is no justice in the world’<br/>
>(Rounds 2013: 271)

>b. *jövök : **nem**  jövök*<br/>
>jövö-k : nem jövö-k<br/>
>come-1SG : NEG come-1SG<br/>
>'I'm coming : I'm not coming'

**Coding.** The value is '1' if existential and standard negation are expressed by different negative markers (as in Hungarian).
