**Does  'who' commonly appear as a relative pronoun used for animates other than humans?**

Relative clauses may be introduced by a pronoun. This question asks, whether the interrogative pronoun 'who' can function as a relative pronoun referring to animates other than humans. For instance, in the Livonian example (1) *kis* 'who' is used for snakes.

(1) Livonian (Viitso and Ernštreits 2012)<br/>
>*ne ūška-d, **kis** jāmsta-bõd*<br/>
>these snake-PL who bite-3PL<br/>
>'these snakes that bite'

**Coding.** The value is '1' if there is an interrogative pronoun 'who' that also appears as a relative pronoun and in addition to referring to humans  it also refers to animates other than humans. The value is '0' if the respective pronoun cannot be found to express both, or a different pronoun is used instead.
