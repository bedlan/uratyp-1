**Can the 3rd person plural verb form and impersonal (passive) verb form be encoded in the same way?**

This question asks whether one and the same verb form can appear in the finite paradigm (i.e. the 3rd person plural verb form) as well as impersonal (or passive) paradigm. For instance, this is the case in Livonian (1a-b) and Veps (2a-b).

(1) Livonian 
> a. *ne **vȯst-īz-tõ** būomvilliz-t luoimõ*<br/>
> 3PL buy-PST-3PL cotton.ADJ-PRT thread.PRT<br/>
>‘They bought cotton thread’<br/>

>b. *Siz mūndakõrd **vȯst-īz-tõ** būomvilliz-t luoimõ* (Viitso 2008: 321)<br/>
>then sometimes buy-PST-3PL cotton.ADJ-PRT thread.PRT<br/>
>‘Then sometimes cotton thread was bought’<br/>

(2) Veps
>a. *hijamo-t **teh-tas** paid-ha, ombel-das* (Grünthal 2025: 258)<br/>
> sleeve-PL make-PRS.PASS shirt-ILL sew-PRS.PASS<br/>
>‘Sleeves are made and sewn onto the shirt’<br/>

>b. *hö **teh-tas***<br/> 
> 3PL make-PRS.PASS<br/>
> ‘They make’<br/>

**Coding.** The value is '1' if one and the same verb form can be used on both occasions.
